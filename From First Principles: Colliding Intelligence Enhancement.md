Integrated from sources: Focus on evidence-based, first-principles levers (e.g., novelty as entropy export; RL as feedback for adequacy). Start with substrate (neural health), build recursion (relational/pattern skills), sustain via loops (challenges/networks).

1. **Relational Reasoning Training** (Core Recursion; from RaiseYourIQ, aligns with neuresthetic mapping)
   - Map relations (e.g., same/opposite) across domains to harden integrations. Evidence: Cassidy et al. (2016) showed IQ gains in kids; Roche et al. (2024) confirmed 10-15 point increases; May et al. (2022) meta-review validates.

2. **Seek Novelty and Challenge Yourself** (Phased Stability; from SciAm)
   - Expose to new activities/tasks, avoiding mastery plateaus. Evidence: Swedish study on dopamine boosts post-training; Haier’s Tetris research: Cortical thickness up, but efficiency drops without novelty—gains 5-10 points via sustained practice.

3. **Reinforcement Learning Loops** (Verifiable Feedback; from Superintelligence blog)
   - Use supervised (text prediction) + RL (rewards for tasks like math/coding). For humans: Apps with feedback (e.g., n-back). Evidence: Jaeggi et al. (2008) initial gains; AlphaGo-style self-play transfers to reasoning—potential 5-12 points, though task-specific per Melby-Lervåg & Hulme (2013).

4. **Physical Exercise and Nutrition** (Substrate Hardening; from RaiseYourIQ/SciAm)
   - Aerobics/Tai Chi for plasticity; fix deficiencies (omega-3s/iron). Evidence: Diamond (2020) meta: Executive function boosts; Fernandez-Rodriguez (2021) review: Maintains cognition, indirect IQ support via health.

5. **Creative Thinking and Networking** (Divergent Integration; from SciAm)
   - Divergent ideation + diverse interactions for perspectives. Evidence: Sternberg’s Rainbow Project: Creative teaching raised grades/analytical scores; networks enable novelty without quantified IQ data.

6. **Do Things the Hard Way + Extended Learning** (Avoid Rigidity; from SciAm/RaiseYourIQ)
   - Skip shortcuts (e.g., no GPS); pursue schooling. Evidence: Brinch & Galloway (2012): 2 extra years yield small IQ gains; personal atrophy examples highlight practice necessity.
